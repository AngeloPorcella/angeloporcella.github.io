## Career and Education Background
I grew up in a small town in Montana. In highschool I held a position in a NIH research lab (Rocky Mountain Laboratories). I leveraged this great experience into a Microbiology Degree at Montana State University. After graduation I got a job at a contract reasearch organization as a lab tech in Bozeman Montana where I worked for three years. I was promoted up to the highest laboratory position (Study Director) but felt like I wasn't operating at my highest potential. This led me to the decision to go back to school for a MS in Computer science, my intention being a focus in Bioinformatics. Quickly I realized my aptitude for more software engineering pursuits and an interest in vulnerability discovery and cyber security. I worked as a graduate teaching assistant for multiple classes and eventually was offered a research position from Dr. Ann Marie Reinhold, a professor I deeply admire. Currently I am performing research in that position in the Software Engineering and Cybersecurity Laboratory (SECL). I'm graduating may 2025 (woo!) and am on the hunt for a job.
